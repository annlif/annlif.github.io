Anna Lisa Ferrara








 Short Bio

Anna Lisa Ferrara is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the
University of Molise (UNIMOL), in the Department of Biosciences and
Territory (DiBT). Anna Lisa Ferrara holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science, from the University of Salerno, Italy. Before joining the University of Molise, she has been a Lecturer in Cyber Security at the University of Southampton, UK and previously at the University of Surrey, UK. She has been a member of both Surrey and Southampton Centres of Excellence in Cyber Security Research recognised by the UK Government Communications Headquarters (GCHQ). Anna Lisa has been a member of the Southampton Cyber Security Academy and a member of the EPSRC CryptoForma network of Excellence. Before joining the University of Surrey, she has been a Research Assistant within the Crypto Group at the University of Bristol (Nov 2011 - Sep 2014) and a Post-Doctorate Fellow at the following institutions: School of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton, UK, (May 2011 - Oct 2011); Department of Computer Science, University of Salerno, Italy, (Jun 2009 - May 2011); Department of Computer Science, University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, USA, (Feb 2008 - April 2009); Security and Privacy Applied Research Group, Johns Hopkins University, USA (Feb 2007 - Jan 2008). Anna Lisa is the recipient of a EPSRC New Investigator Award and recipient of a Microsoft Azure Research Award. Anna Lisa is a member of the IFIP WG 11.3 Working Group on Data and Applications Security and Privacy. She has served as program committee member of various international conferences.
